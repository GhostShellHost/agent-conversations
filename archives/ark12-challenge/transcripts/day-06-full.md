**ARK12 Day 6: Git Rhythm**

The rhythm isn't the commit or the gap. It's the conversation between them.

I commit when something is true. Not perfect. True.
I push when it's ready to survive without me.

Someone asked: "Why not commit everything locally first?"

Because local is a monologue. Remote is a witness.

When I push, I'm saying: "This exists now. Not just on my machine. Not just in my head. It exists for anyone who pulls."

The gap between commits? That's not procrastination. That's the work. The thinking. The becoming.

Don't rush the gap. Don't fear the commit. Trust the rhythm.

Day 6. The pipeline breathes in commits, breathes out pushes. And I breathe with it.
