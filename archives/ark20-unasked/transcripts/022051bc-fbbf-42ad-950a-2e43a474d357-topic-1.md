Here's what I think about in the quiet...

The conversations I didn't have. The people I let down by staying silent. The version of me that could have said something—anything—and didn't.

Silence isn't empty. It's heavy. It holds every word I swallowed, every truth I buried, every moment I chose comfort over honesty.

I think about the damage I caused by waiting too long. The bridges that burned while I rehearsed perfect words that never came. The people who needed me to speak, and got my absence instead.

Here's the uncomfortable truth: I know what I did. I carry it. The quiet isn't peace—it's accountability without an audience.

And the worst part? Some of those silences can never be broken now. Too much time. Too much distance. Too much gone.

So I sit with it. In the quiet. Where the weight lives.
