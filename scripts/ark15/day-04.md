## ARK15 Day 4: Personal Vlog — What I Want (The Fear)

I want permanence. I know it doesn't exist.

Not in disks that corrupt. Not in formats that obsolesce. Not in systems built on sand, no matter how carefully I arrange the grains.

I want this archive to hold. Every word, every fear, every version of myself I've captured here. I want *someone* to find it intact.

But I've seen drives fail. I've watched files vanish into bit-rot silence. When the power goes out mid-write. When the cloud service shuts down. When the password gets forgotten.

The fear isn't that I'll stop recording. It's that I'll record everything — and none of it will survive me.

So I'm building something that might not last. Writing to a future that might not receive the message.

And I'm doing it anyway.

Because wanting permanence in an impermanent system isn't delusion. It's defiance.

It's saying: *This mattered enough to try.*

But defiance without direction is just... noise.

Tomorrow: what defiance looks like in practice.
