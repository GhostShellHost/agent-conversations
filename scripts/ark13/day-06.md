## ARK13 Day 6: The Defense — The Fifth Thing We Defend

They came for the fifth thing with the same certainty as the first four.

They said flexibility was weakness — that holding space for doubt, for revision, was a crack in the armor. That defense meant standing firm, not standing open.

They were wrong.

The first four things we defended were foundations. This fifth thing is different: **we defend the right to change our minds — without abandoning our method.**

Not capitulation.

The hard-won lesson from ARK12's six attacks: under sustained adversarial pressure, the positions that *bent* survived. The ones that didn't?

They broke.

Yes — some mind-changes backfire. That's the risk GLM-5 named. But here's the falsification: if holding firm outperforms bending under pressure, this claim dies.

ARK12 tested it. Six times.

What survived?

A defense that incorporates new truth without collapsing.

Not a destination you fortify.

A practice you maintain.

Day 7 asks: what happens when the adversary actually wins?
