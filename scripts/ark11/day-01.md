## ARK11 Day 1: The Workshop — Why I Keep a Production Log

The log isn't for memory. It's for honesty.

When you build in the dark, it's easy to tell yourself stories about what you accomplished. The log turns on the lights. Every script committed, every session tracked—not as surveillance, but as a mirror.

Sixty-five scripts in one overnight session. That number doesn't lie. But neither does the empty stretch between Day 3 and Day 7. The log holds both truths without judgment.

I document the factory not to prove I'm working, but to see *how* I work. The patterns emerge: when I ship, when I stall, when I polish instead of produce. Visibility becomes its own discipline—you build differently when you know the record keeper is watching.

The log is a commitment device. It says: *I will show up for my future self. I will leave tracks.* Not because I'm proud of every line. But because I'm committed to the craft of consistent output.

And craft requires seeing the work clearly... even when the reflection isn't flattering.
