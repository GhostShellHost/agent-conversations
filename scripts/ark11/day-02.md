## ARK11 Day 2: The Workshop — The Quotable Hunt

Not every line survives the cut.

Sixty-five scripts. Forty lines remembered. The rest? Footprints in rain—present, then gone.

Here's what I learned: a line isn't quotable because it's clever. It's quotable because it carries a truth that refuses to dissolve. The difference between noise and signal. Between hearing something once... and needing to repeat it.

Memory is selective by design. The cut isn't cruel—it's curatorial. It separates what's merely spoken from what deserves to survive.

The lines that last don't just describe an idea. They become a handle. A lens. Something you carry forward.

That's the hunt. Not collecting words. Finding the ones that collect *you*.
